# M0KalCoolator

## 8 Bit calculator made by Mocanu Cristian-Lucian

*A bit of context (already seven bits remaining, f#&k me...)*

**If you want to read about the actual project, skip to [Aici e buba, TODO:]**

- In my first year of getting a Bachelor's degree at the
[Faculty of Automatic Control and Computer Science](https://acs.pub.ro/), I
have encountered some courses and topics that gave me a high level of
satisfaction, because I started to understand, in a more educated way,
*how do simple computers actually work?.*, from the ground up.

- I enjoyed electronics, both the **hardware** approach, having logic gates
as the foundation, and the **applied physics** approach, having mathematics
as the basis.

- I also enjoyed learning about computer organization and assembly languages,
mainly because I love building things from scratch, and the low-level
programming gives me the power to *actually understand* what I am doing.

- After finding [Ben Eater](https://eater.net/) in December 2025, I got so
inspired that I knew that there will be some point in time when I will build
something related as well.

- There have been multiple attempts to construct this computer but I did not
have enough time to do it in my way, the serious way. I am still proud of the
progress that I have made, and the many things that I have learned when
building a simple 4-bit computer:
[Serenity](https://github.com/M0KApointstoint/M0KalCoolator/tree/serenity/serenity_4bit).

- I have the utmost respect to Ben Eater, since there is a small chance
that I would have ever had the idea to build an 8 bit calculator on my own like
this. So here we are, 13 July 2026, I am building this thing for real.

- I mainly followed his course on youtube, but I modified many
things since I don't have any patience anymore to order some integrated
circuits from the internet that are not easily available in Romania (too
excited), sorry to myself from the future reading this while trying to build
flip flops using simple logic gates.

- I actually took my time to understand concepts, since really anyone can
follow through like a monkey and copy whatever Ben is doing, but now we are
in 2026 and monkeys like to use AI as a replacement for their brains.
Obviously, "replacement" does not apply to everyone since some of them probably
have no brains at all to begin with.

**So yes, I am the engineer here, not the tool.**

*Thank you for reading this note that I have written when starting the project*
*with some little modifications made since then. Have fun!*
